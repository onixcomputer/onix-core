## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Syncoid replication service
A `zfs-replication` tag SHALL be created and applied to aspen1 and aspen2. It SHALL configure syncoid to replicate selected datasets from aspen2 (primary) to aspen1 (replica) on a scheduled basis.

#### Scenario: Scheduled replication
- **WHEN** 6 hours pass since the last replication
- **THEN** syncoid sends incremental ZFS snapshots of `rpool/home` from aspen2 to aspen1 over SSH

#### Scenario: Initial full sync
- **WHEN** syncoid runs for the first time (no common snapshot exists on aspen1)
- **THEN** a full send of the latest snapshot is transferred, establishing the replication baseline

### Requirement: Replication dataset scope
Syncoid SHALL replicate only `rpool/home` from aspen2 to aspen1. System datasets (`root`, `nix`, `var-log`) SHALL NOT be replicated because they are reproducible via NixOS rebuild.

#### Scenario: Nix dataset not replicated
- **WHEN** syncoid runs its replication job
- **THEN** `rpool/nix` on aspen1 contains only aspen1's own nix store, not aspen2's

### Requirement: SSH authentication for syncoid
Syncoid SHALL authenticate between aspen2 and aspen1 using a dedicated SSH key pair generated by clan vars. The private key SHALL be accessible only to the syncoid service user.

#### Scenario: Key generation
- **WHEN** `clan vars generate --machine aspen2` runs
- **THEN** an ed25519 SSH key pair is generated for syncoid, with the private key encrypted by SOPS

#### Scenario: Authorized key on replica
- **WHEN** aspen1 deploys
- **THEN** the syncoid public key from aspen2 is in the authorized_keys for the syncoid receive user on aspen1

### Requirement: Replication over Tailscale
Syncoid SHALL connect to the replica machine via its Tailscale hostname (`iroh-aspen1`), not a LAN IP, to work regardless of network topology.

#### Scenario: Replication across networks
- **WHEN** aspen1 and aspen2 are on different subnets
- **THEN** syncoid connects via `iroh-aspen1` through Tailscale and replication succeeds

### Requirement: Replication monitoring
Syncoid replication failures SHALL be visible in `systemctl status syncoid-rpool-home.service` and the journal.

#### Scenario: Replication failure alert
- **WHEN** syncoid fails (network issue, SSH auth failure, pool not imported)
- **THEN** the systemd service enters failed state and `journalctl -u syncoid-rpool-home` contains the error details
